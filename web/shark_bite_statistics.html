Shark Bite Statistics

Sharks have a fearsome reputation, but the numbers tell a calmer story. The Florida Museum of Natural History keeps the International Shark Attack File, the longest-running scientific record of shark bites, and its 2024 report described the year as "exceptionally calm."

How many bites really happen

Worldwide in 2024 there were just 47 unprovoked bites, down 22 from the year before and well below the ten-year average of about 70. Only four of those bites were fatal. An unprovoked bite is one where a person did not initiate contact with the shark.

Where they occur

The United States consistently records the most unprovoked bites, with 28 in 2024, roughly 60 percent of the global total. Florida alone accounted for 14, more than any other state, with eight of those in Volusia County, often nicknamed the shark bite capital of the world. Australia was a distant second with nine bites and no fatalities.

Who gets bitten

Surfers made up about a third of all bites in 2024. They tend to be in turbid, wave-rich water where bait fish gather and visibility is poor, so sharks occasionally make mistakes.

Keeping it in perspective

Of more than 500 shark species, only about 13 are known to bite humans with any regularity. The odds of being hurt by a shark are tiny compared with far more ordinary hazards, and humans remain a vastly greater danger to sharks than the other way around.

- Florida Museum of Natural History, International Shark Attack File
