How to Protect the Ocean

Sharks can only thrive in a healthy ocean, and protecting that ocean does not require living on the coast. Because most pollution reaches the sea from human activity on land, the choices we make far inland matter too. Here are simple, everyday habits that help.

Cut down on waste and plastic

Reduce what you throw away, buy less single-use plastic, and carry a reusable bag. Less plastic on land means less plastic drifting through the water, where it never fully breaks down.

Watch what goes down the drain

Use less water so that excess runoff and wastewater do not flow into the ocean, and choose nontoxic products. Dispose of herbicides, pesticides, and cleaning products properly instead of pouring them away.

Choose sustainable seafood

Picking responsibly sourced seafood helps ease the pressure of overfishing on sharks and the species they depend on.

Travel and boat with care

Use fuel-efficient transport, carpool, or ride a bike. On the water, anchor in sandy areas well away from coral and sea grass, and respect "no wake" zones so fragile habitat stays intact.

Get involved

Volunteer for beach and community cleanups, and support efforts to protect your local watershed. Small actions, repeated by many people, add up.

- NOAA National Ocean Service
